Embargo Period Explained

As described in the Service Description, each experiment (dataset) becomes publicly available 3 years after its creation.
For example, an experiment created on 5th May 2018 is ready for public release on 6th May 2021.

We lagged in the public release of existing data, but we are starting the automatic process now in 2024.
The releases will be made in tranches organized by the creation date (2017, 2018, 2019, 2020) over the course of the next 12 months, and will then be made on a continuous basis.

Paid, Increased Embargos

The default 3-year embargo period can be extended to 5 or 10 years by paying a yearly subscription fee.

Each experiment created during the 'paid' year will have the release date automatically increased to 5 (or 10) years in the future.
For example, if you paid a subscription for a 5-year embargo in 2023, all the data created in that year won't be released before 2028. However, data created after 2023 will only have the default 3-year embargo unless you pay for a new subscription.

What About Existing Data?

We are aware that by not releasing the data automatically after 3 years, we created a false sense of 'security' that the data would remain private, which might have prevented users from paying for longer embargo periods.

For this reason, we offer the option to pay for higher-tier subscriptions for past years as well.

Subscription Prices

  • Subscription with a 5-year embargo: £120 per year
  • Subscription with a 10-year embargo: £240 per year

You can pay upfront for multiple years.

The prices are calculated based on our running costs per experiment and assume that each user creates 10 experiments per year on average.
